## Ideas / Strategy

* Replace generic gameplay titles with curiosity-driven formats
* Focus on high-drama moments (1 HP clutch, 1v4 fights, last zone panic)
* Build challenge-based content:

  * No Healing Challenge
  * No Scope Only
  * Loot Only From Drops

## Thumbnail Strategy

* Main concept: 1 HP survival situation
* Left: player with low HP (red screen effect)
* Right: multiple enemies rushing
* Text: “1 HP 😳”
* Style:

  * Dark background + red highlights
  * Blurred background, sharp subject
  * Zoom on face or health bar

---

## Why It Works

* Curiosity gap increases clicks (“1 HP”, “Only 1%”)
* Survival situations trigger emotional engagement
* Ego trigger (“Can I do this?”) increases watch time
* Shorts bring new audience into long-form content

---

## How To Execute

1. Focus each video on ONE strong moment
2. Start with peak moment preview (first 5 seconds)
3. Remove slow gameplay and looting sections
4. Use fast editing:

   * cuts every 2–4 seconds
   * zooms and sound effects
5. Keep video length between 6–10 minutes

---

## Pro Tips

* Use Hinglish commentary for relatability
* Add tension music before fights
* Use pattern interrupts every few seconds (zoom, subtitle, sound)
* Highlight key moments early to improve retention

---

## Performance Focus

* CTR target: 6–12%
* Retention target: 40–60%+
* First 10 sec retention: 70%+

---

## Key Insight

Low CTR is not a content problem.
It is primarily a packaging problem:

* weak titles
* weak thumbnails
* lack of curiosity

Improving packaging will significantly increase impressions and clicks.
